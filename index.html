Each piece is rectangular with 2 rectangular parts;

Each rectangular part has dotted values ranging from 0 to 6;

There are no two equal pieces, the piece 🁓 is equal to 🁟;

In total there are 28 pieces;

Each piece has a certain amount of points (the sum of its dots);


At the start the pieces are shuffled and distributed in 3 groups:
Player hand;
Opponent hand;
Stack;

The player that has the 🁡 piece (highest value piece) starts the game, placing it in the center of the game board;


During the game each player will take turns playing;

Played pieces form a game line, new pieces must be placed in either ends of the line;

To place a piece next to another the touching rectangles must have the same amount of dots;


If either one of the players doesn't have any playable piece, he must use the stack. The player will keep on taking pieces until it is possible for him to play;

The current player can and must skip his turn if and only if the stack is empty;


The game ends when:
A player has no more pieces (he wins);
The game is closed and no player can play;

The game is closed when both the game extremities have the same value and 7 other pieces have that value;

The winner is the player with the least amount of points in his hand (the points are the sum of each value in the 2 rectangles in one piece);
